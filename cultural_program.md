  <div style="flex:2;">
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;">Monday</h3>
    <p>
      On Monday, a visit to <em>Palazzo Bembo</em> will introduce the 
      <a href="https://europeanculturalcentre.eu/about/aboutus" target="_blank" rel="noopener">European Cultural Centre</a> 
      that hosts the exhibition <em>Time Space Existence 2025</em>, 
      which is on display in the framework of the 
      <a href="https://www.labiennale.org/en/architecture/2025" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Venice Architecture Biennial 2025</a>: 
      <em>Intelligens. Natural. Artificial. Collective.</em>  
      The introduction by architect Hadi El Hage will establish the first links between science and art, which will be a thread across the whole week.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex; gap:20px; align-items:flex-start; flex-wrap:wrap; margin-bottom:30px; flex-direction:row-reverse;">
  <img src="/images/cultural_2.jpg" alt="Redentore" style="flex:2; max-width:500px; border-radius:8px;">
  <div style="flex:2;">
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;">Thursday</h3>
    <p>
      On Thursday, we will walk to the <em>Giardini del Redentore</em>, a historical garden maintained 
      and managed by the Venice Gardens <a href="https://almadalcofondazione.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Foundation</a>, 
      under the direction of its president Adele Rerebaudengo. This extraordinary place, blending nature and culture, 
      has just reopened to both visitors and residents. Professor Donatella Calabi will tell us of this restoration project 
      and of other initiatives that testify to the vitality of a city despite overtourism, 
      which she has reviewed in her book <em>Venezia è viva. Venice is alive. Venise est vivante</em>.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex; gap:20px; align-items:flex-start; flex-wrap:wrap; margin-bottom:30px;">
  <img src="/images/cultural_3.jpg" alt="Palazzo Franchetti" style="flex:2; max-width:500px; border-radius:8px;">
  <div style="flex:2;">
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;">Friday</h3>
    <p>
      On Friday, <em>Sala del Portego</em> of Palazzo Franchetti, the seat of the 
      <a href="https://www.istitutoveneto.it/flex/cm/pages/ServeBLOB.php/L/EN/IDPagina/9" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Istituto Veneto di Scienze, Lettere ed Arti</a> (IVSLA), 
      will host the public lecture on Venice and the Lagoon, exploring the solutions to protect and maintain 
      the unique cultural and environmental heritage face to the unprecedented challenges posed by overtourism 
      and sea level rise. The theme will be introduced by Professor Andrea Rinaldo, president of IVSLA, with 
      <em>Considerations on Venice in a Changing World</em>. Professor Francesco Musco, president of the research
      consortium CORILA, will present <em>Venice and the Regulated Lagoon</em>. 
      <strong>Being a public event, lectures will be in Italian with English slides 
      and translation of salient points.</strong>
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="display:flex; gap:20px; align-items:flex-start; flex-wrap:wrap; margin-bottom:30px; flex-direction:row-reverse;">
  <img src="/images/cultural_4.png" alt="Concerts" style="flex:1; max-width:300px; border-radius:8px;">
  <div style="flex:2;">
    <h3 style="margin-top:0;">Saturday</h3>
    <p>
      On Saturday, the <a href="https://almadalcofondazione.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Alma Dal Co Foundation</a> 
      and the <a href="https://www.conservatoriovenezia.eu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Benedetto Marcello Venice Conservatory</a>, 
      host in <em>Sala Concerti</em> a public 
      <em>Conversation on Science and Music</em>, with the participation 
      of philosopher Massimo Cacciari, musicologist Sandro Cappelletto, neuroscientist Daniela Perani, 
      Cardinal Matteo Zuppi and the doctoral students of the Venice Conservatory. The students will 
      perform their own original music composed for the occasion. This extraordinary encounter between
      eminent figures of science, culture and religion and young researchers offers a unique opportunity 
      to bridge Alma Dal Co’s two major passions. 
      <strong>This public event will be open to the citizens of Venice and held in Italian 
      but music speaks to everybody.</strong>
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
